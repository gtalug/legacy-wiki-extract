Emulators: Mac:Executor and Windows:Wine.

Presented by Laszlo Herczeg 

Date: Sun, 26 Nov 95 00:09
From: las@light-house.com (Laszlo Herczeg)
To: lug-friends@light-house.com
Subject: December meeting announcement


Dear Linuxers,

The next meeting of the Toronto Linux Users Group will be held
at 7 pm in the North York Public Library on Wednesday, November 29, 1995.

Of the most exciting recent Linux projects, I wish to discuss
"Executor", a Macintosh binary emulator, and the latest version of
Wine, a windows emulator.

At the meeting we will also be giving away some Linux CD sets,
courtesy of Adam Richter at Yggdrasyl Computing.

Location:   Meeting Room 2
            North York Public Library
            5120 Yonge Street


How to Get There:

           By the subway: take the Yonge subway
           to the "North York City Centre" stop.
           The library is at the southwest end of
           the mall connected to the subway.

           By automobile: The library is located at 5120
           Yonge Street, just a bit north of Sheppard Ave.
           Ample parking is available one block west of the
           library, on Beecroft Street. As well, there
           is underground parking accessible from
           Parkhome Ave.



Everyone is welcome to attend. Be there!


  Regards,
