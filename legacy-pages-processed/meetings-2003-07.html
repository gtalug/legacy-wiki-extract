Data Loss and Recovery with a focus on Linux.

Presented by Ron Austin and Laslo Orto.

TLUG meetings are held at UofT on the second Tuesday of each month at 7:30 pm.


Date
July 8, 2003
Time
7:30 pm


Topic
Data Loss and Recovery with a focus on Linux.
Speakers
Ron Austin and Laslo Orto
Laslo Orto, Senior Systems Specialist Laslo is the young genius of the team. He is quick to assimilate new situations, extrapolate from known systems and file formats and design custom solutions for successful data recovery.
Ron Austin, VP Business Development Ron Austin holds a BA in Computer Science from York University and graduated with distinction. Ron began his IT career as a programmer/analyst then moved into sales, marketing and eventually general management gaining many years experience at the senior executive level of technology companies including serving as President & CEO of Globelle Corporation.
Description
  • The manifestation of data loss and its physical and logical causes.
  • Preventing data loss and mitigating its effects.
  • Recognizing data loss and distinguishing it from other computer problems.
  • What to do and what not to do during a data loss emergency.
  • The highly technical, in-lab process of retrieving data from problem digital media.
  • The challenges of working with inaccessible tape backups and server data-sets that span multiple hard drives.
  • Recovering Linux systems.
  • The use of Linux as a recovery tool.
  • Technical Level: Audience Appropriate
