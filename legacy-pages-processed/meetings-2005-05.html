Linux-based soft PBX projects and their connection to the existing telephone networks.

Presented by David Mandelstam.

TLUG meetings are held at the University of Toronto on the second Tuesday of each month at 7:30 pm.

Date
Tuesday May 10, 2005
Time
7:30 pm
Topic
Linux-based soft PBX projects and their connection to the existing telephone networks.
Speaker
David Mandelstam (CEO Sangoma)
Description
The talk will focus on the problems associated with the connection of software-based PBX applications to the POTS (plain old telephone network) and how to overcome them.
Speaker Bio

David Mandelstam is the founder, President and CEO of Sangoma Technologies. Before starting Sangoma, David ran Entropy Control, which was involved in energy engineering, and was engineering VP of Solartech, an energy conservation company. Prior to that, David worked for Spar Aerospace and South African Airways. David holds a B.Sc. in mechanical engineering from the University of Witwatersrand in South Africa, an M.Sc. in aerodynamics from the Cranfield Institute of Technology in the United Kingdom and a B.Comm. from the University of South Africa.
