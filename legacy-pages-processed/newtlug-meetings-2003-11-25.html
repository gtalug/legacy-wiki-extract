Badges: email PWArmstrong to pre-register

Date: Tues Nov 25th. Time: 7-9:30pm


Title: Legal Issues Affecting Canadian IT Professionals

Presenter: Emir Aly Crowne Mohammed B.A., LL.B., LL.M. (Information Technology & Telecommunications Law) 

               - Director of Intellectual Property & Legal Affairs

               - LL.M. Candidate in Intellectual Property Law at
Osgoode Hall

               - Lecturer with the University of Toronto (School of
                 Continuing Studies) in Legal Issues in E-Commerce and

Business Law

Topics: An overview of some of the legal issues facing current and future information technology professionals in Canada.

Emir's talk will include topics such as: 

               - elements of contract law and the acceptance of
on-line
               contracts/software licences: shrinkwrap, clickwrap and
               browsewrap

               - privacy, ISP liability and spam

               - overiew of intellectual property (trade-marks,
patents,
               copyright, and confidential information), including
related
               aspects of work product ownership and non-disclosure

agreements.
