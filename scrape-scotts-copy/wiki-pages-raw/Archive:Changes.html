2005 - April

Fri Apr 29
  • Sitemap > Culture was added to contain all the phrases and terms and such which naturally end up being described in a wiki.
  • Resources > Linux distributions was created, but is still stub-like.
  • Consultants > Frauerpower! was added.
  • Resources > Perl was added.


Thu Apr 28
  • Psema4 (stub) was added.
  • Linux was updated and appears to be very nicely rounded out with the addition of a Certification section.


Wed Apr 27
  • More tweaking is being done behind the scenes.
  • Footnoting was adjusted to make footnotes easily searchable.


Tue Apr 26
  • Village pump was created, as a central space to chat about the wiki itself.
  • Adding a meeting's Template:Meeting was created, as an easy way to add a new entry in Meetings.
  • Other minor adjustments.
  • Linux was updated with a list of resources.


Mon Apr 25
  • Moved some of the changes to the Archive. (Page was getting too long)
  • Efforts continue. Past meetings are slowly being ported from the website-proper.
  • Sitemap and the heirarchical sectioning concept was tidied up. It ought to be pretty easy to maintain the concept of heirarchy and item description. I hope. =)
  • The main page of http://tlug.ss.org was ported over into various sections.
  • Mailing lists and IRC were ported from the other setup.
  • Events was ported and tidied up. "Current events" was absorbed into it. Related organization was done.
  • The main tlug.ss.org now references this wiki. We're live!


Sun Apr 24
  • Organization continues..
  • Consultants was created.
  • Systems Software was created.
  • Meetings' topics continue to be ported.
  • Manifesto was created, although that page certainly needs to be rewritten.
  • Various other things..


Sat Apr 23
  • This place was set up.
  • My thanks to Bill and Drew for coming to the rescue in a pinch. -- Sy / (talk)
  • Sitemap, Meta and To do were created.
  • Meetings was created.
  • People was created.
  • History was created.
  • About was created.
  • MediaWiki was created, and has some rough notes for new editors.
  • Sy wrote The wiki as a guiding light for open software development
