Executive Meeting - 2009-05-25

[edit]

Regular Business

  • Delegation of gtalug.org
    • Chris has it redelegated; we still need another nameserver!
    • To Do: Drew has set up dns.gtalug.org, and ns.gtalug.org
    • It's done now! (2009-05-26)
  • New GTALUG server status
    • Upgrade to new version of Mediawiki apparently works
    • Miles to update the GTALUG template for the new version; need to update the template first
    • Apparently the MySQL DB is getting regularly corrupted; it is speculated that dumping the DB and loading it onto "gold" machine is likely to resolve this...
    • Next tale: To Migrate to MailMan as mailing list manager... Should think about this once the wiki's dealt with.
      • Need to have an archiving answer
      • Need automating of managing addresses
    • Would really like to have the front page be much more dynamic, removing the ancient history from it
      • Discuss what ought to be done about improving richness of web pages
      • Need to reorganize the front page
  • Drew recommends... Bob Niven has a content management system supporting payment via PayPal. We should arrange to have a copy of this system installed so that we may automate receipt of funds.
    • Requires setting up vendor account with PayPal and connecting this to the bank account
    • Targeting end of summer
    • Followup: Need to consider what we wish to do with this.
      • Buttons, shirts, ???
    • Defer further on CMS to June

    • Chris bringing projector

New Business

  • ICANN Membership
    • Got the membership information - to fill in
    • If Gord doesn't see any objections, he'll send out what he has by end of month
    • ISOC would be neat too, but no rush...
    • Plan for a presentation to GTALUG at the October meeting.
  • NewTLUG
    • More on status???
    • Nothing this month, but hopefully next month
  • Linux and Education
    • Meeting last week @ LinuxCaffe
    • Plans to put on a course
  • Heckling issue
    • Definition?
    • Enforcers?
    • Board discussion tends to agree that this isn't a huge problem, and further, that the activity seen is not very frequently actually of a "heckling" nature.
    • The good speakers tend to be able to cope well, and the kibbitzing has been recognized as even being valuable by some speakers
    • It is considered to be up to speakers to choose their favoured rules of engagement.
    • We need to make sure speakers are aware that:
      • Meetings are interactive
      • They can pick their rules
    • We should have a web page declaring the rules of engagement, with phrasing such as...
      GTALUG membership are not interested in marketing presentations, and reserve the right to interact with speakers.
    • Regrettably, we have some that get overenthusiastic about hearing themselves talk. They need to be smacked :-)
  • Wiki Spammers
    • Needs the wiki upgrade
  • Need a review of the GTALUG Board list
    • There are a bit too many people on the list
