Topic

Adventures in Modern Publishing

[edit]

Description

Over the last several years, the nature of publishing has changed. And quite radically. Th electronic book (ebook) has gone from being a curiosity to becoming a major force in publishing what used to be known as the printed word.

And the growth of the market for ebooks, as well as ebook readers, has:

1. Opened the door for writers to more easily and efficiently self publish their work 2. Given companies another channel in which to distribute documentation, marketing material, and more

While PDF has long been the choice for distributing ebooks, it’s not the best format for ebook readers, handheld devices, tablets, or even desktop and laptop computers. So, what is? EPUB. And there are a number of ways in which you can publish your ebooks in the EPUB format using free and Open Source tools.

In this presentation, Scott Nesbitt will look at:

  • What EPUB is
  • How an EPUB file is structured
  • Why EPUB is the logical choice for distributing ebooks
  • A few of the uses of EPUB (it’s not just about novels!)
  • A number of free and Open Source tools for creating ebooks
  • Some of the pitfalls of using these tools, and how to get around them

A version of this may be found at Publish Your Own Books With FOSS Tools

[edit]

Speaker

Scott Nesbitt wears many hats: writer, blogger, editor, speaker, consultant. A long-time user of free and Open Source software, Scott has been exploring how to use free and Open Source tools write and publish since he started using LaTeX in the early 1990s. After much experimentation, he recently published his first ebook (Getting Organized with Google's Tools). You can learn more about the book here: http://scottnesbitt.net/ebooks/Google_Tools/GoogleTools.html
