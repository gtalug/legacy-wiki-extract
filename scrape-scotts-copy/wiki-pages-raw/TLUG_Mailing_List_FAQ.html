TLUG Mailing List, Frequently Asked Questions

[edit]

Is there an archive of this list?

There is no official archive! But Gmane and Mentby have non-official archives.

[edit]

Can I post technical questions to the list?

Yes, the list is for all announcements, discussions and anything else you can think of involving Linux and Linux users in the Toronto area.

[edit]

Is there a Toronto group for people who are new to Linux?

TLUG tries to be welcoming to new users, but sometimes we can get ahead of ourselves. As a response to this, NewTLUG has been in operation since at least 2002. They're a great bunch and lots of people visit both TLUG and NewTLUG. Visit http://tlug.ss.org/wiki/NewTLUG_meetings for more information.

If you're a new user and you can't make it out to TLUG. TLUG meetings are preceded with an informal question period, and there are many opportunities to meet some very helpful people. Most of us would be happy to discuss any installation or configuration questions you might have.

[edit]

How old/young are the people of TLUG?

Every age from highschool students to retirees. Feel free to stretch the age bracket further.

[edit]

Where are the meetings?

Visit http://www.gtalug.org/wiki/Main_Page for details of the next meeting location.

[edit]

How do I post?

After you subscribe, send your post to tlug@ss.org. Your post will appear on the list. Note that if you 'CC' or Carbon Copy a person who is already a member of the list, they will receive two copies of your post.

[edit]

I can't post to the list, why?

To protect the list from SPAM, a person must be subscribed to the list in order to post to it. If you already subscribed to the list, check your mail program to be sure that the email address in your reply-to field is the same as the email address you subscribed with.

[edit]

How do I unsubscribe/subscribe to the list?

  • To me&Body=subscribe subscribe to TLUG, send an email with the word "subscribe" in the body to tlug-request@tlug.ss.org In a few minutes, you will receive a confirmation message. Follow the instructions in the message and you're on.
  • To unsubscribe from the list, send the word "unsubscribe"

If you have any trouble subscribing or unsubscribing, an email with the word "help" in the body to the above address. A complete summary of commands will be sent to you.

[edit]

What is the average volume of messages on the list?

At the time of writing, the list usually receives between 0 to 20 posts a day. There are typically 5 or 10 posts in a day.

[edit]

Did you pull those numbers out of a hat?

Yes.

[edit]

Can I send files to the list?

While it is possible to send files to the list, please do not do it! There are a few very good reasons for this:

  • Internet Service Providers (ISPs) generally have limitations on the size of a person's mail file. If you send a file to the mailing list, you risk flooding those mailboxes.
  • Some people have slow network connections. If files begin to appear on the list, it would take these people an inordinate amount of time to download their mail.

If you have a file to distribute, you should post it to your website and send the URL to the list. If you don't have a website, there are many free accounts available at sites such as http://wikisend.com

[edit]

Why are people irritated that I posted in 'HTML'?

HTML is the language of the World Wide Web. If your mail program allows you to change the colour, font, size or any other aspect of the text you are writing, you are probably posting to the list using this document markup standard.

Plain text is just that. Plain text. HTML more than doubles the size of your post, increases the time it takes to download messages in the group, and makes it difficult for people with non-HTML enabled mail programs to read.

Consult the documentation provided with your email package to disable this feature.

[edit]

What is a flame/flamewar/SPAM?

Flames are heated discussions which are usually personal and almost certainly off-topic. They can get big very quickly, and that's called a flamewar. If you see a message on the list which you personally object to, please exercise restraint. Remember that this is a forum about Linux in Toronto and that nobody subscribed to read arguments.

SPAM is unsolicited bulk communication of any sort. This means posting messages such as get-rich-quick schemes and chain letters -- no matter how convincing they may be. If I haven't said it yet, we don't want SPAM. Nobody wants SPAM.

[edit]

Where do I learn more about etiquette on mailing lists?

There are many people who have written about Internet etiquette, everything from emotionicons to posting in all caps. The best way to find out about this stuff is to use one of the Internet search engines and search for the word "netiquette"

There is also a book written on the subject, it is one of the most clear and concise documents I have seen on the subject. It has also received some very good reviews. Just skimming over the online version will make you an expert.

  • The full version of the online book is available at http://www.albion.com/netiquette/book/index.html
[edit]

I'm an employer, can I post a job offering to the list?

In order to keep traffic to a minimum, job listings may be posted provided that they are infrequent, related directly to Linux in the Toronto area and direct all inquiries to some place other than the list. We do not appreciate postings from employment agencies or "headhunters". If you have any particular questions about this policy or the suitability of a particular job listing, please contact me at mgjk@idirect.com.

[edit]

I would like to advertise my company's products or services on your list. May I?

Please don't. There's nothing wrong with putting links to your company's website in your sig, or telling somebody who's trying to resolve a problem that you have a product or service which might help them. But please do not blindly throw advertisements into the forum. I'm sure the last thing you want to do is tick off the people you are trying to sell to.

[edit]

I heard RedHat, ucLinux and other corporations have presented before your group, whom should I speak with to have my corporation present?

Matthew Rice, matt@starnix.com is a good person to get in touch with to set up this sort of event.
